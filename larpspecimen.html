✣ a display font in the British gothic tradition ✣
larp display is a bold titling font with a strong historic, fantasy character, ideally suited for branding & displays. The design retains the colour of black-letter but its letterforms are still familiar to us today unlike its German cousins Fraktur, Schwabacher etc. The name 'LARP' comes from 'live action role-play', reflecting on the often fantastical nature of reviving type from old manuscripts; a typeface often 'roleplays' a reference point, a period, a culture, whilst still being very much a product of its time. In this way, it joins a long lineage of similar design intentions, like that of William Morris's 'Troy Type' or Will H. Bradleys 'Bradley' and so on.

larp is a semi-faithful revival typeface which mixes, mainly, two scripts which were widely in use in England during the Gothic period: ''textualis prescissus'' and ''anglicana''. The former is often “considered to have been at the top of the hierarchy of Gothic scripts”According to Derolez in his "Palaeography of Gothic Manuscript Books"(2003), the latter was unique to England, where it was developed in 14C, in response to “two factors: the increasing demand for books, and the increase in the size of the works to be copied.”Parkes, in "English Cursive Bookhands"(1969). By combining the form of two different Gothic scripts, the aim was to create a palaeographicaly accurate hybrid, which points at a period and a place, but which never actually existed: a ‘fantasy’ revival.

To help the letterforms feel faithful to the period, the design process was kept entirely traditional, with a mixed use of nib, pencil & brush.
