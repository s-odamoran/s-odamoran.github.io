This page was written and put together during the Corona Virus lockdown in the UK, in response to the Info & Systems ‘Acts of Publishing’ brief.

The main idea behind the site is what digital tech - and the platforms and cultures it births - does to our sense of time. The Corona Virus pandemic has served to exacerbate this warping of time, increasing the hours of our days that are mediated via screens. I’m someone who finds it hard to get away from the endless rhythm of the internet, the infinite scroll – this page is an expression of that struggle. An endless stream of homogenised, familiar content, often making you guilty for the inaction that it has lulled you into in the first place.

The internet and digital tech are just tools, and so this is not some kind of luddite argument for regression, or an appeal to analogue. Without the internet, no doubt a lot of us would find the self-isolation much bleaker, the communication, entertainment, and education it offers us is indispensable.
However, it's important to remember that the platforms providing this stimulation are active participants in shaping our world and not simply neutral mediators of data, as they often come across as. To control the digital infosphere is to control the narrative of the real world, the distinction between the two is mostly meaningless. To have your attention is to have your time, the most valuable asset you have - it's important to spend it wisely.
